Alt Text Guidelines

Examples of good and bad Alt Text and common errors that go along with the process.

 

General Guidelines: 

  • Be clear and concise, no more than 2 sentences

  • Describe the image accurately and avoid vague phases

  • Make sure the context supports the content

  • Do not use image, picture, link, multimedia. It will already do this for you.

  • Do not put alt text for decorative images

  • Use punctuation marks since the screen reader will read it to the user

  • Provide captions for videos and long descriptions for charts and graphs if important

  • Do a usability test with a screen reader

  • Prioritize clarity and do not use keywords often

 

Examples of Bad Versus Good Alt Text:

  • Example #1: Picture of a horse with a pretty sunset

    • Bad: "A picture of an animal"

    • Good: "A brown horse with a sunset in the background.:"

  • Example #2: Multiple people in a work setting talking to each other

    • Bad: "Figures in a room".

    • Good: "Multiple people in a work conference room talking to one another."

  • Example #3: A birthday party with a cake and multiple presents

    • Bad: "A picture of bright objects in a room"

    • Good: "A birthday celebration with multiple presents on a long table."

  • Example #4: A grassy field with rain falling down

    • Bad: "Dark setting with a field."

    • Good: "A green long grassy field with raining falling down at night."

    Example #5: A graduation ceremony with multiple people sitting down

    • Bad: "A ceremony that people sit at."

    • Good: "A graduation ceremony where graduates and parents are sitting in their seats."

 

Common Errors: 

  • Not putting punctuation where it is needed. People with disabilities need to be able to follow along and move at their own pace.

  • Making it more than 2 sentences long. People need to gauge what the most important takeaway is from your media piece to be able to move forward.

  • Putting alt text for decorative media. You have to understand the intended purpose of your media so your users can also follow along.
