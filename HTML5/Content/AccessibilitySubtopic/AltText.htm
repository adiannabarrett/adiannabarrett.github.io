Accessibility Topic - Alt Text

What Alternative Text is and the purpose of why we use it in everyday life.

 

What is Alt Text and the Purpose of Alt Text?

Alt text is when you provide a description or context. Alt text is attached to an image, chart, or graphical image that makes it accessible to all users. The purpose of alt text is to help users with low or impaired vision, not loading documents, screen readers, and trouble with hearing. It should be 1-2 sentences which will be key points of your image that you want to convey. You are going to want to end alt text with a period, since it is a sentence. In those specific 1-2 sentences, specify if it is a logo, icon, cartoon, painting, etc. but do not include the word "image" or "graphic". Screen readers already announce what type it and also announce that they are links.

You want to make your alt text intentional, and convey the most important message of that specific image or content. Empty alt text is fine, but it is only used when an image does not convey relevant or important context, or just used for decorative purposes.

 

Why does Alt Text Matter?

Alternative text matters because it continues to meet the standards of accessibility and help a wide range of people. Persons with visual impairments, screens that will not load, or just general technical limitations. It provides context to relay information directly. It also helps with search engines in web platforms since it uses alt text to be able to understand images, links, and multimedia. This can enhance your overall performance of your website. It overall makes it more inclusive and can always adapt to different devices and environments.
